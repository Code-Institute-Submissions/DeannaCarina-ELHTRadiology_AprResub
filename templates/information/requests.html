Requests

Radiology requests are much the same as a prescription that you would get from your General Practitioner. You would need to discuss your symptoms/illness with your doctor/consultant and they will decide if a particular radiology examination is appropriate for you and your care.

Unfortunately you are not able to attend the radiology department and get an examination without first seeing a doctor or referring physician who has put a radiology request onto the system that can be accessed by the radiology department.

You are able to get a radiology request from the following (this is not an extensive list):
  • General Practitioners (GPs)
  • Consultants involved in your care
  • Nurses that have had extra training
  • Sonographers (ultrasound radiographer) in certain circumstances
  • Reporting radiographers in certain circumstances
  • MRI radiographers in certain circumstances
  • Nuclear Medicine radiographers in certain circumstances
  • Physiotherapists in certain circumstances
  • Podiatrists in certain circumstances
  • Occupational Therapists in certain circumstances
Anybody who is not a doctor or registered with the GMC is classed as a 'non-medical referrer (NMR)' and it is necessary for these professionals to have undergone extra training to ensure the necessity of the examination and the safety of their patients. If your examination has been requested by an NMR, the member of staff carrying out your examination may need to do some extra checks before they bring you in for imaging.

As per IR(ME)R 2020 regulations, it is necessary for any patient due to undergo an examination involving radiation to have an examination request submitted by a registered practitioner (referrer), this can be a doctor registered with the GMC or a registered practitioner that has undergone extra training (NMR). The members of staff undertaking your examination (operators) must ensure the request the referrer has put forward is justified under IR(ME)R regulations i.e. that there is a valid reason to carry out your examination and that the benefits outweigh the risks.

Without an examination request, we are unable to determine whether the examination you need is necessary for your care.

When you have seen a referring physician and they have decided you need a radiology examination, they will put an electronic request on the system for the radiology department to access. They will also give you a paper copy. Once you have this, you can then book an appointment on the ELHT RBS using the details on your examination request.

You do not have to bring your request with you on the day of your examination, however it may be helpful to have it with you in case the radiology systems go down (an unfortunately frequent occurrence) as we would be unable to carry out your examination without it and you may need to come back to the department at a later time/date.

After you have had your radiology examination, you are able to keep the examination request for your records, or you can dispose of it as you would any other confidential waste when you are home.
