Department

When you arrive in the radiology department, we aim to start your radiology examination within 15 minutes of your appointment time. If you are waiting longer than this, please let a member of reception staff know and they will enquire as to the nature of the delay. All radiology departments also cater to the care of hospital inpatients on an urgent basis, x-ray and CT also cater to the needs of emergency medicine - because of this, it may be the case that we need to delay your appointment time to ensure the safety and wellbeing of another patient. There may be other unforeseen delays such as machine breakdowns.

We will do everything possible to ensure a quick and comfortable visit to the radiology department, however if you feel you have waited longer than necessary, please don't hesitate to discuss this with a member of staff on the day of your visit, or by using the contact form on this website.

Within the radiology department we have a large number and range of staff members including (but not limited to):
  • Radiology assistants
  • Radiology assistant practitioners
  • Radiographers
  • Senior radiographers
  • Reporting radiographers
  • Consultant radiographers
  • Radiologists
Your examination may be carried out by one, or a combination of these staff members depending on the examination you are having. All of our staff have been trained to the highest standard and are continually reviewed by the department (as well as their professional body if applicable) to ensure their skills and training are kept up-to-date to provide a safe and effective service to their patients.

While we aim to have male and female staff available at all times, this can not always be guaranteed. A number of our radiology sites are ran by a single member of staff, in these cases it is not possible to guarantee a female member of staff, although you may ask for a female chaperone, they will not be able to carry out your examination.

Please be assured that all of our members of staff are trained to the highest standard and will always have your care and dignity at the forefront of their minds, they will not ask you to remove clothing unless absolutely necessary for your examination, and in these cases they will provide you with an x-ray or hospital gown to maintain your dignity.

If you ask for a female member of staff to carry out your examination, we will try our best to provide this service to you, but it may not always be possible due to departmental pressures.
