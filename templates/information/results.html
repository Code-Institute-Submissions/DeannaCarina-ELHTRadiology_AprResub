Results

X-RAY
  • If you have had an x-ray as part of your visit to A&E or urgent care, your examination will take priority in the reporting stage. Our plain-film emergency reporting time for between the hours of 9am and 4pm is 1 hour, however your referring emergency doctor will have experience of diagnosing a range of pathologies via x-ray.
  • Our reporting time for GP plain-film referrals is 7-10 days for orthopaedic images, and 10-14 days for all other images and pediatrics (under 16s).
  • If you have been referred from an outpatient department for plain-film images, these images will not be reported by our in-house reporters, instead these will be reviewed and commented on by your own consultant.
CT SCANS
  • If you have had a CT scan as part of your visit to A&E or urgent care, your examination will take priority in the reporting stage. We have radiologists on site between the hours of 9am and 5pm to report on any emergency CT scans, and have access to radiologists out-of-hours when needed.
  • If you have been referred via outpatients or via your GP, the reporting time for these scans is usually within 2 weeks, however if you have found you have waited longer, please contact your referring physician and they can follow up with the radiology department.
ULTRASOUND, DEXA & MAMMOGRAPHY
  • Results from Ultrasound scans, Dexa scans and Mammograms usually take a week to get back to your referring physician for non-urgent cases.
MRI SCANS, FLUOROSCOPY & NUCLEAR MEDICINE SCANS
  • Results from MRI scans, Fluoroscopy and Nuclear Medicine scans usually take 2 weeks to get back to your referring physician for non-urgent cases.

Usually if you have been referred via emergency medicine or urgent care, you will get the results on the day (before you go home) from the person that requested the examination (usually the doctor or nurse in A&E or urgent care), you will not get the results from the member of staff who takes your images.

If you have been referred by your GP to plain-film x-ray for a suspected fracture of a bone, you will get the results on the day from the member of staff that took your images, however this can take up to an hour, as we must prioritise urgent cases. If you have a confirmed fracture, we will then escort you to urgent care to be assessed and treated.

For outpatient referrals, if you are going back to the outpatient department after you have visited x-ray, then the referring physician should discuss your images with you (though this isn't always the case), if you aren't going back to the outpatient department then you will usually get the results the next time you see/speak to your referring physician which can sometimes be up to 4 weeks.

For the majority of examinations, you will not be able to get the results on the day, it takes extra training to be able to diagnose and read x-rays/scans which the majority of radiology staff have not had.

For routine GP referrals, your GP will give you the results of your examination.
